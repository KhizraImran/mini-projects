Our Story

Three generations, one kitchen.

Not just a restaurant — a living archive of heritage, love, and memory.

The Beginning

Where It All Started

Long before Tina's Kitchen had a name, there was a family kitchen — recipes taught by hand, passed from grandmother to daughter, generation after generation. Nothing written down. Everything remembered. The smell of lemongrass, the sound of the mortar and pestle, the colour of turmeric staining the wooden board.

Tina Opens Her Doors

Tina brought those same unaltered recipes to 70 Lebuh Kimberley, George Town — determined to keep every flavour exactly as she remembered it. No shortcuts. No substitutions. Just natural ingredients, traditional technique, and a deep respect for every dish she'd been taught to make.

70 Kimberley
Kampung Soul

A Kampung Style Philosophy

Every dish at Tina's Kitchen carries the spirit of Kampung Style cooking — humble, thrifty, and deeply flavourful. Nothing wasted. Everything intentional. Dishes like our Kiam Hu Kut Gulai connect directly to Peranakan, Hokkien and Malay roots that stretch back centuries in Penang's remarkable cultural heritage.

The Next Generation

Today, Tina's daughter works alongside her in the kitchen — learning every recipe, every technique, every unwritten secret. She didn't learn from a cookbook. She learned by watching, by tasting, by standing at the stove. The story continues exactly as it always has: by hand, by memory, by love.

Still Going
Today

A Home Away From Home

Guests don't just come for the food — they come for the warmth. Our team, including a wonderful member of staff who is deaf and mute and always greets every visitor with the loveliest smile, makes everyone feel like family. That feeling? It's not an accident. It's who we are.

"We don't cook to impress. We cook to remember."
