Commission a Vehicle

Noir Motors was conceived from a simple but radical belief: that the automotive industry had become too afraid to be extraordinary. Obsessed with volume, constrained by regulation interpretation, and blinded by focus groups, the major manufacturers had drifted into a world of homogeneity dressed as innovation.

We exist to be the antidote. We produce fewer vehicles in a year than most manufacturers produce in a morning. Every one of those vehicles is a commission — a conversation between our atelier and a single client — and it will never be repeated.

This is not nostalgia. We are not building the cars of the past. We are imagining the cars that should exist — that could only exist — if commercial constraints were removed entirely and replaced with a single directive: make it perfect.

Perfect to drive. Perfect to inhabit. Perfect to be seen in. Perfect to think about at four in the morning, when you cannot sleep, and all you can hear is the memory of that engine.

Timeline

Six Years of
Absolute Intent

2019

The Founding

Édouard Marchand, formerly aerodynamics lead for two consecutive Formula 1 World Championship-winning constructors, leaves motorsport with a vision and gathers eleven specialists to a warehouse in Geneva. Noir Motors is registered. Nothing is announced.

2020

Development Begins

Work begins on the monocoque chassis for what will become the Phantom Noir. The first carbon fibre tub is laid in March. Nine months later, a running prototype completes its first kilometre in the atelier courtyard at 2am, witnessed only by the team. No cameras. No press. Only engineers and the sound of something new in the world.

2021

Phantom Noir Debut

The Phantom Noir is revealed privately to 12 selected individuals at an invitation-only event in Geneva. All 12 place commissions before leaving the building. The automotive press, who are not invited, begin writing about a car they have not seen from photographs that do not yet exist.

2022

Obsidian R & First Deliveries

The first Phantom Noir vehicles are delivered. Simultaneously, the Obsidian R track-road derivative is announced. A lap record attempt at the Nürburgring Nordschleife is conducted privately. The time is not released. The team know what it means.

2023

Séraph V Concept

Work begins on our most ambitious project: the Séraph V. A twin-turbo V10 producing over 1,000 horsepower. Active aerodynamics. A target weight below 1,200kg. The team consider it impossible. They build it anyway. Eclipse GT and Vantis S enter development.

2024

Séraph V Launch & Arcane One

The Séraph V is delivered to its first eight clients. Six months later, the Arcane One — our most radical creation, limited to 12 examples worldwide — is revealed to a waitlist of 340 interested parties. Development of a sixth model, designation unknown, begins in silence.

2025

The Complete Collection

All six models in full production. A new atelier facility — twice the current size — begins construction in the Jura foothills. The commissioning waitlist exceeds 18 months. We do not intend to reduce it.

What We Stand For

Principles
That
Never Change

Honesty of Purpose

Every element of a Noir Motors vehicle exists for a reason. We do not add visual complexity for its own sake. We do not fit components that serve no function. If it is there, it works. If it works, it is there.

Singularity of Commission

We refuse to produce identical vehicles. Every commission begins with an extended conversation. Every vehicle ends with a client who owns something that has never existed before and never will again.

The Patience of Excellence

We take 2,400 hours to build each vehicle. We take 18 months to develop each design iteration. We take as long as the work requires. Excellence cannot be rushed. We have no intention of trying.

Absolute Transparency

Our clients see their vehicle at every stage of construction. They understand exactly what they are commissioning and exactly how it is made. There are no shortcuts. There is nothing to hide.
