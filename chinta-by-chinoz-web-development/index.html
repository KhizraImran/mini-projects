Our Story

A Hidden Gem
Behind the Plants

Tucked along the leafy lanes of Jalan Kemuja in Bukit Bangsar, Chinta by Chinoz is more than a restaurant — it is a living memory. Born from the hearts of those who built Chinoz on the Park, one of Kuala Lumpur's most treasured dining institutions, this intimate space carries forward a quarter century of culinary devotion.

Here, grandmother's recipes meet thoughtful plating, and every dish is an act of remembrance — of spice-warmed kitchens, laughter around the table, and the deep, unhurried flavours of authentic Malay home cooking.

Our Heritage

The Story of
Chinta

Founded With Heart

A Legacy
Reimagined

For 24 remarkable years, Chinoz on the Park stood as one of Kuala Lumpur's most beloved dining institutions — a place of warmth, flavour, and genuine hospitality. When that chapter closed, it was not the end, but a transformation.

Founded by Teng Wee Jeh and Sandra Samad, Chinta by Chinoz was born from the deep desire to honour what they had built — not through nostalgia alone, but through something deeply personal: the flavours of Malay home cooking that Sandra grew up with.

Settled into a characterful shophouse on Jalan Kemuja, Bukit Bangsar, Chinta is an intimate sanctuary. The name itself — chinta, the Malay word for love — says everything about what drives every dish that comes out of this kitchen.

"We cook the way our mothers cooked — with patience, with memory, and with an abundance of love."

Our Menu

The Kitchen

Flavours of a
Malay Home

Every dish is cooked fresh each day — guided by the rhythms of the season, the traditions of Sandra's kitchen, and the belief that the best food is always made with unhurried care.

Nasi Campur

The heart of Chinta — steamed fragrant rice paired with your choice of daily rotating lauk. A complete meal, a complete feeling.

Nasi Campur

Aromatic steamed rice with a curated selection of the day's best lauk — rendang, sambal, sayur, ikan, kerabu, and more. No two days are exactly alike.

Daily Special

Nasi Campur — Premium

Choose three or more lauk from the daily selection, including premium cuts and chef's signature sides. A feast worth lingering over.

Recommended

Nasi Campur Hari Raya Style

On festive occasions, we bring out the full grandeur — ketupat, lemang, rendang, and all the ceremonial lauk of a Malay celebration table.

Seasonal

Main Dishes

Slow-cooked, deeply spiced, and full of character — our mains are the soul of Malay cooking as it should be.

Ayam Rose

A signature Chinta creation — tender chicken cooked in a delicate, mildly spiced rose sauce, fragrant with lemongrass and a touch of coconut cream.

Chinta Original

Ayam Rendang Hijau

Green rendang chicken — an aromatic slow-cook of free-range chicken in a blended paste of green chillies, galangal, kaffir lime, and turmeric leaf. Herbaceous and hauntingly good.

House Favourite

Daging Salai Gulai Lemak

Smoked beef in a rich, golden gulai lemak — a Negeri Sembilan–style curry that balances the smoky depth of salai beef with the gentle sweetness of coconut milk.

Slow-Cooked

Sambal Tumis Udang Petai

Prawns and petai (stink beans) stir-fried in a punchy sambal tumis that is fiery, fragrant, and unmistakably Malay. A beloved classic that demands a second helping of rice.

Must-Try

Mee Rebus Johor

Thick yellow noodles in a hearty, slightly sweetened gravy enriched with sweet potato and aromatics — a Johorean street-food classic elevated into a full dining experience.

Southern Heritage

Sides & Salads

Light, bright, and deeply traditional — the salads and sides that complete any Malay table.

Kerabu Pucuk Paku

Young fern shoots tossed in a vivid kerabu dressing of toasted coconut, shrimp paste, calamansi juice, and bird's eye chilli. Refreshing, textured, and deeply aromatic.

Salad

Papadums & Condiments

Crisp homemade papadums served alongside rotating condiments — sambal belacan, acar rampai, budu dip — each prepared in-house with Sandra's own touch.

Complements

Kuih & Desserts

A sweet finale — traditional Malay kuih and desserts made daily, by hand, from recipes that have been in the family for generations.

Kuih Gula Hangus

A dark, caramel-tinged steamed kuih made with burnt sugar, coconut milk, and glutinous rice flour — intensely fragrant and slightly bitter-sweet in the best possible way.

Traditional

Seri Muka Telang

A two-layered glutinous rice kuih — the base sticky and rich, the custard top infused with butterfly pea flower (bunga telang) to a stunning natural indigo. As beautiful as it is delicious.

Showstopper

Chinoz Layer Cake

The legendary Chinoz layered cake — a rich, buttery Sarawak-style kek lapis that has been a quiet icon since the Chinoz on the Park days. Sold whole or by the slice.

Legacy Dish

Drinks

Cooling, nostalgic, and perfectly paired with the bold flavours of Malay cooking.

Sarbat Pulau Pinang

A classic Penang-style rose syrup drink — iced, sweet, and floral, with a hint of spice. The taste of a hot afternoon in old Georgetown, bottled in a glass.

Signature Drink

Long's Ais Krim Soda

A frothy, fizzy ice cream soda inspired by the old kopitiam tradition — a creamy scoop of vanilla floating in sweet soda water, a childlike delight for all ages.

Nostalgic Classic

Teh Tarik Chinta

Our house-made teh tarik — strong brewed black tea pulled to a silky, frothy perfection with full-cream condensed milk. The essential accompaniment to every Malay meal.
