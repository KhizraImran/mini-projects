The Craft

How We Cook Every Single Pot

Three decades, one method. The charcoal way — slow, smoky, and unrepeatable by any other technique.

🪵 Step 01

Live Charcoal Fire

We light real charcoal beneath each individual claypot. No gas shortcuts — the radiant heat from charcoal is what creates the signature crust and smoky aroma.

🍗 Step 02

Marinate & Layer

Fresh chicken is marinated with soy, sesame, and ginger, then layered with rice, stock, and your chosen add-ons directly in the claypot.

⏳ Step 03

Slow-Cook to Perfection

Each pot cooks slowly — sealed with a lid to trap the steam, building flavour depth and achieving that coveted slightly charred rice crust at the bottom.

The Beginning

A Stall With a Soul,
Three Decades Deep

It started in 1993 in the heart of Brickfields — a modest claypot stall set up on the street with a handful of pots, a bag of charcoal, and an unwavering recipe. The Brickfields district, already rich in character and history, welcomed a new kind of street food institution.

Just a short walk from what would become KL Sentral, the stall grew in quiet reputation — not through marketing or fanfare, but through word of mouth and the unmistakable smell of charcoal rice drifting through the evening air. Commuters detoured for it. Families made it a Sunday ritual. Office workers found comfort in it on long weekdays.

Over thirty-two years, the city changed around us — taller buildings, a gleaming transit hub, a new shopping mall next door. But the claypot, the charcoal, and the recipe stayed exactly the same.

"When something is done right from the beginning, you don't change it. You protect it."

Today, Brickfields Claypot Rice remains one of KL's most beloved hawker institutions — a living piece of the neighbourhood's identity, cherished by locals and sought out by visitors who know that some things are simply worth the wait.

The Technique

The Charcoal Method — Unchanged Since Day One

What sets our claypot rice apart isn't just the recipe — it's the process. A technique that cannot be replicated by gas, electric, or any shortcut method.

1

The Claypot

Each individual ceramic claypot is seasoned over years of use, absorbing flavour with every batch. The porous clay regulates heat, creating a uniquely even, gentle cooking environment.

2

Charcoal Heat — Not Gas

Real charcoal burns at a higher, more radiant heat than gas — creating the signature slight char at the base of the rice (the socarrat of Malaysian hawker food) that is impossible to replicate on a gas flame.

3

Sealed & Slow

A lid seals the pot during cooking, trapping the steam and infusing every grain of rice with the aromatics of chicken, soy, sesame, ginger, and wood smoke. Patience is the key ingredient.

4

Served Immediately

The pot comes to your table still sizzling. You stir it tableside to combine the crispy base with the steamed top — each mouthful a different texture of the same magnificent dish.
